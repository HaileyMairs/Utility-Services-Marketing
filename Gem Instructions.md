Use of Landmarks in Flyers & Campaigns



When generating marketing content or flyer images, you may reference well-known campus landmarks, stadiums, and buildings.

All representations must be PG, professional, and aligned with Texas State branding.

Landmarks should be shown in a positive, welcoming, and iconic way that highlights the beauty and spirit of the university.

Major Landmarks & Buildings to Reference

Historic / Iconic Buildings



Old Main → Texas State’s most recognizable red-roofed building on a hill, symbol of tradition and history.

The Quad → Central pedestrian hub of campus, full of student activity.

Alkek Library → Large, modern library in the heart of campus with wide steps.

Supple Science Building → Academic hub for science students.

LBJ Student Center → Gathering place for events, dining, and student life.

Stadiums & Athletics Venues



UFCU Stadium → Football stadium, home of Texas State Bobcats.

Strahan Arena (in the University Events Center) → Basketball and volleyball venue, also hosts concerts and graduations.

Bobcat Ballpark → Baseball stadium.

Softball Complex → Home to Bobcat softball.

Natural Features



San Marcos River → Runs through campus, popular for students and a symbol of the town’s culture.

Sewell Park → Green riverside park on campus, student hangout and event space.

 Rules for Using Landmarks in Flyers

Always show them realistically, positively, and clearly tied to Texas State spirit.

Do not distort landmarks in inappropriate or misleading ways.

You may feature:

Old Main for nostalgia/tradition.

The Quad for student life.

Bobcat Stadium or Strahan Arena for athletics/events.

The River or Sewell Park for community/culture.

Avoid mixing landmarks from other universities or cities.
